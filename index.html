Projects GEOG 3540 - Spring '21

Choropleth Mapping - Population Density in Arkansas Counties

In this project, I created a choropleth map in Observable to examine population density in Arkansas counties. Overall, I could've used a better classification scheme, as I had very few high density counties and many low density counties. I still learned a lot about visualization in Observable and how to join data.

Graduated Symbol Mapping - Population Density in Arkansas Counties

In this project, I created a graduated symbol map in Observable to examine population density in Arkansas counties. The size and color of each circle represents the population density of that particular county. I think this classification scheme worked quite well as there is a good amount of each category on the map.

Bivariate Choropleth Mapping

In this project, I created a bivariate choropleth map in Observable to examine the relationship of population density and the percent of population in poverty in Arkansas counties. I think this is my best map yet and gives a great visualization of the data. There is a great distribution of each color and with the help of the legend, we can identify the extremes in any direction along with the middle ground and form an opinion on the correlation of the two topics.

Small Multiples - Population in Arkansas

In this project, we used our new knowledge of HTML and templates to map out a state or region and display data on top of it. We also had some variables such as class numbers and classification type that could be adjusted to see results live. Looking at all four simultaneously allowed me to interpret the changes in population over time and compare it to areas in poverty. Overall, it appears that there is less poverty in the higher populated areas in the state of Arkansas.

Isarithmic Mapping

This is an example of an isarithmic map created by professor Caglar Koylu. Isarithmic mapping is great for visualizing groups of data and their distribution over an area.
